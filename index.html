About Me

Hello! I am Parikshit Mahajan, an MTech (Research) student at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore in the Department of Computational and Data Sciences. My academic journey is driven by a passion for scientific machine learning and the intersection of physics and data.

My research focuses on Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) and Stochastic PINNs for solving partial differential equations (PDEs) under uncertainty. I am particularly interested in developing robust and efficient methods for uncertainty quantification in scientific computing.

I am always eager to collaborate on interdisciplinary research and contribute to the academic community through open-source projects and publications.

Research Interests

  • Scientific Machine Learning (SciML): Integrating machine learning with scientific computing for modeling and simulation.
  • Neural Operators for Stochastic Differential Equations: Data-driven operator learning for solving SDEs and high-dimensional stochastic systems.
  • Uncertainty Quantification: Bayesian methods, Bayesian PINNs, and probabilistic deep learning for quantifying uncertainty in scientific models.
  • Stochastic PDEs: Data-driven and physics-based approaches for solving PDEs under uncertainty.
